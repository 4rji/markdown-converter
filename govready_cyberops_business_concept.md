helping small businesses, local organizations, and government contractors improve their cybersecu-
rity readiness, automate repetitive workflows, and prepare for government contracting opportuni-
ties.

| 3   | Why      | This          | Business | Makes |     | Sense |     |     |     |
Small businesses and contractors often want to sell to the government but lack the internal cyber-
security maturity, documentation, and operational efficiency needed to compete confidently. Many
of these organizations do not need a large enterprise security platform. Instead, they need practical
help with the basics: asset inventory, access control, backups, logging, policies, incident response
| planning, |     | and efficient | workflows. |     |     |     |     |     |     |
| --------- | --- | ------------- | ---------- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
Thisbusinessisdesignedtofillthatgapbyofferingpracticalcybersecurityservicesandlightweight
automation solutions that are affordable, understandable, and directly useful.
The business model aligns well with current public guidance. NIST provides Cybersecurity
Framework 2.0 resources specifically for small businesses, including a quick-start guide for small
and medium-sized businesses with modest or no cybersecurity plans in place. CISA also provides
voluntaryCybersecurityPerformanceGoalsintendedtohelpsmallandmedium-sizedorganizations
prioritize high-impact cybersecurity actions. In addition, SAM.gov explains that entities must
register if they want to directly bid on government contracts or apply for federal assistance.

4 Target Customers
• Small government contractors
• Businesses preparing to register or compete for government contracts
• Local government offices
• Small municipalities
• Nonprofits receiving grants or public funding
• Small schools or education programs
• Managed service providers that need cybersecurity or automation support
• Small technical teams with repeated manual processes
5 Primary Value Proposition
We help small organizations become more secure, more organized, and more efficient
by combining cybersecurity readiness with practical workflow automation.
The company does not only identify security problems. It also helps simplify operations by
building small tools and workflows that save time and make security easier to maintain.
6 Service Pillars
6.1 Pillar 1: Cyber Readiness
This service area focuses on helping organizations understand and improve their security posture.
Example deliverables:
• Cybersecurity readiness assessment
• Asset inventory review
• MFA and password policy review
• Backup and recovery review
• Basic vulnerability scan and prioritized remediation list
• Network and endpoint security checklist
• Incident response starter plan
• Cybersecurity gap analysis report
• Executive summary for non-technical leadership
2

6.2 Pillar 2: Process Automation
This service area focuses on identifying slow, repetitive, or manual workflows and replacing them
with simple automation.
Example deliverables:
• Internal dashboards
• Report generators
• Ticket or support templates
• Log parsing scripts
• File organization tools
• Email or document automation
• Workflow forms that generate reports or checklists
• Small web applications for internal use
• Data cleanup and formatting tools
6.3 Pillar 3: Lightweight Monitoring
This service area helps small organizations gain basic visibility without deploying a large enterprise
SIEM.
Example deliverables:
• Centralized syslog collection
• Basic security dashboard
• Alerts for important events
• Monthly log review summary
• Basic network visibility
• Documentation of monitored systems
• Recommendations based on observed activity
6.4 Pillar 4: Government Contractor Preparation
This service area helps businesses organize the cybersecurity and operational documents often
needed when preparing for government contracting.
Example deliverables:
• Security policy templates
• Acceptable use policy
• Access control policy
